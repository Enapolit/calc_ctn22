America’s solar energy industry has survived pandemic-driven global supply chain issues and shipping disruptions.But there’s one obstacle the solar industry might struggle to overcome — mounting prices.

Prices for residential photovoltaic (PV) systems have risen nearly 50% over the past year as the prices of component materials like polysilicon, steel, glass and copper continue to rise.

The situation could be a knife-edge for the thriving residential solar industry that has emerged along America’s sunbelt. That is because the long-term cost benefit of solar energy for consumers is what entices many homeowners to make the switch to solar.

But now, with prices rising and the end of government subsidies for home PV systems on the horizon, consumer interest in solar energy could wane.

On the West coast, the average monthly cost of full-time child care trails closely behind that of the Northeast at $1,013. That comes out to $12,159 a year.

Southeast

The Southeast, where cheaper, home-based child care providers are common, has the lowest child care costs in the nation. The average monthly cost of full-time child care coming in at $682. That comes out to $8,187 a year.

Southwest

In the Southwest, the average monthly cost of full-time child care is $781. That comes out to $9,366 a year.

Northeast

The Northeast has the highest child care costs in the nation, with the average monthly cost of full-time child care coming in at $1,150. That comes out to $13,803 a year.

Midwest

In the Midwest, the average monthly cost of full-time child care is $941. That comes out to $11,287 a year.
